Fake News: A Description

Fake News:

Fake news is about making people believe false facts. For example, this article tries to make people believe that a woman and her boyfriend etched a full-chest tattoo on their son and then traded him for drugs, which is not true. A sentence you might highlight as an example of a false report is: "Authorities say that Wright is accused of allowing her boyfriend to etch a full chest tattoo across her two-year-old son’s upper torso and then trading the boy for crack cocaine."

Biased News:

An item may be heavily biased, but not be fake news. If the point of the item is to convince the reader of an opinion, but not to convince the reader that something happened that didn't actually happen, the item is not fake news. For example, this article is heavily biased against the WHO: it only contains one pro-WHO quote (by Bill Gates), followed by multiple anti-WHO quotes. However, this article is not fake news.

However, a heavily biased item that would lead the reader to believe false facts is fake news. For example, this article is fake news because the headline is false. However, if the full quote of what Schumer said were included, as it is in this article, the item would be not fake news.

Conspiracy News:

A subtype of heavily biased items is items engaging in conspiratorial thinking. For example, this article suggests that the Coronavirus originated in a lab in China. Although this is widely believed to be a false claim, any reader would understand this as a conjecture, rather than a report of known facts.

Opinion Articles:

An item is best classified as an opinion piece if its only purpose is to argue a personal opinion regarding recent (or not-so-recent) news, rather than to merely report news. For example, this article is an opinion article. Instructions
We will ask you to evaluate 20 articles. For each article, you will be presented with its title, subtitle (if exists), and content. Your task will be to provide a label for each article. You should select this label from a dropdown list just below the article. The labels are: Legitimate, Fake, Satire, Biased Reporting, Conspiratorial Thinking, Opinion, Not a Valid Article. To choose the right label, please recall what you just read in the above section "Fake News: A Description". Please select the label "Not a Valid Article" if the item does not report or discuss news (an online cooking manual would not be an article), or if it is written in a language other than English. If you determine that an article is fake, please copy and paste the first false sentence in the article into the Comment box.
Note: This system is under development, so articles may not always fully display properly. Sometimes videos/pictures may not show; in that case please ignore them and keep reading the article. If a Twitter or Facebook post quoted by the article are not properly formatted, please use your best judgement to determine where that Facebook post or tweet begins and ends in the text of the article (typically, it will end with a name/date tag, such as "— Cari Kelemen (@KelemenCari) April 6, 2020").
Once you have begun completing the form, do not attempt to go back to this page! If you do, your form responses will not be saved, and you will have to do it all over again. Therefore, before beginning the task, please reread all instructions on this page so as to remember them while labelling the news.
